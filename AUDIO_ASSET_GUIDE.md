# Audio Asset Sourcing Guide

**For**: Bahnfish Phase 6 - Asset Integration
**Target**: ~800MB of audio assets
**Status**: Asset sourcing in progress

---

## Overview

This guide helps you source, create, or purchase the audio assets needed for Bahnfish. The audio system framework is complete; it just needs the actual audio files.

**Total Required**:
- 8+ music tracks (~200-300MB)
- 100+ sound effects (~50-100MB)
- 50+ ambient layers (~300-400MB)

---

## Option 1: Hire a Sound Designer/Composer (Recommended)

### What to Provide Them

Give them these documents:
1. **Scripts/Audio/README.md** - Technical specifications
2. **AUDIO_ASSET_GUIDE.md** (this file) - Asset list
3. **GAME_DESIGN.md** - Game overview
4. **VIDEO_ANALYSIS.md** - Tone and atmosphere reference

### Estimated Cost
- **Full audio**: $2,000-$5,000 (professional)
- **Music only**: $500-$1,500
- **SFX only**: $500-$1,000
- **Budget option**: $500-$1,000 (semi-professional)

### Timeline
- **Full audio**: 4-8 weeks
- **Music only**: 2-4 weeks
- **SFX only**: 2-3 weeks

### Where to Find Sound Designers
- **Fiverr**: $50-$500 per track (wide range of quality)
- **Upwork**: $500-$5,000 for full game audio
- **SoundBetter**: Professional composers
- **Reddit**: r/gameDevClassifieds, r/INAT
- **Discord**: Game audio communities

## Music Track Specifications

### Required Tracks (8 minimum)

#### 1. Menu Theme
- **Duration**: 2-3 minutes (seamless loop)
- **Mood**: Atmospheric, mysterious, inviting
- **Tempo**: 60-80 BPM
- **Instrumentation**: Ambient pads, subtle piano
- **Reference**: Main menu ambience
- **Format**: OGG, 44.1kHz, 16-bit, Stereo

#### 2. Day Theme
- **Duration**: 3-5 minutes (seamless loop)
- **Mood**: Calm, peaceful, relaxing
- **Tempo**: 70-90 BPM
- **Instrumentation**: Acoustic guitar, piano, light strings
- **Reference**: Cast n Chill vibes
- **Format**: OGG, 44.1kHz, 16-bit, Stereo

#### 3. Dusk Theme
- **Duration**: 2-3 minutes (seamless loop)
- **Mood**: Melancholic, transitional, slightly tense
- **Tempo**: 65-75 BPM
- **Instrumentation**: Strings, cello, subtle electronic
- **Reference**: Sunset ambience
- **Format**: OGG, 44.1kHz, 16-bit, Stereo

#### 4. Night Theme
- **Duration**: 3-5 minutes (seamless loop)
- **Mood**: Tense, ominous, horror undertones
- **Tempo**: 50-70 BPM
- **Instrumentation**: Low drones, dissonant strings, subtle percussion
- **Reference**: Dredge night music
- **Format**: OGG, 44.1kHz, 16-bit, Stereo

#### 5. Dawn Theme
- **Duration**: 2-3 minutes (seamless loop)
- **Mood**: Hopeful, new beginning, relief
- **Tempo**: 75-85 BPM
- **Instrumentation**: Piano, light orchestral, gentle synth
- **Reference**: Morning sunrise
- **Format**: OGG, 44.1kHz, 16-bit, Stereo

#### 6. Fishing Theme
- **Duration**: 2-4 minutes (seamless loop)
- **Mood**: Active, rhythmic, engaged
- **Tempo**: 90-110 BPM
- **Instrumentation**: Rhythmic percussion, melodic lead
- **Reference**: Active gameplay music
- **Format**: OGG, 44.1kHz, 16-bit, Stereo

#### 7. Shop Theme
- **Duration**: 2-3 minutes (seamless loop)
- **Mood**: Upbeat, social, friendly
- **Tempo**: 100-120 BPM
- **Instrumentation**: Accordion, light jazz, upbeat
- **Reference**: Port/town music
- **Format**: OGG, 44.1kHz, 16-bit, Stereo

#### 8. Boss/Chase Theme
- **Duration**: 2-3 minutes (seamless loop)
- **Mood**: Intense, action, danger
- **Tempo**: 120-140 BPM
- **Instrumentation**: Heavy percussion, intense strings, synth
- **Reference**: Horror chase scene
- **Format**: OGG, 44.1kHz, 16-bit, Stereo

### Optional Tracks

9. **Event Theme**: Special events (Blood Moon, festivals)
10. **Credits Theme**: End credits music

## Ambient Layer Specifications

### Location-Specific Ambients (13 locations × 3-4 layers = 50+ files)

Each location needs 3-4 ambient layers:
1. **Base layer**: Core ambience (always playing)
2. **Weather layer**: Rain, wind, storm variants
3. **Time layer**: Day/night variations
4. **Special layer**: Location-specific sounds

#### Example: Calm Lake
1. **calm_lake_base.ogg** - Gentle water lapping (loop, 60s)
2. **calm_lake_rain.ogg** - Rain on water (loop, 45s)
3. **calm_lake_night.ogg** - Night insects, frogs (loop, 90s)
4. **calm_lake_birds.ogg** - Daytime bird calls (loop, 60s)

#### Example: Deep Ocean
1. **deep_ocean_base.ogg** - Underwater ambience (loop, 120s)
2. **deep_ocean_storm.ogg** - Storm waves (loop, 60s)
3. **deep_ocean_night.ogg** - Eerie underwater (loop, 90s)
4. **deep_ocean_whale.ogg** - Distant whale calls (loop, 180s)

#### Example: Bioluminescent Cavern
1. **cavern_base.ogg** - Cave ambience, drips (loop, 90s)
2. **cavern_water.ogg** - Underground water (loop, 60s)
3. **cavern_biolum.ogg** - Magical twinkling (loop, 120s)
4. **cavern_wind.ogg** - Cavern wind (loop, 60s)

**Format**: OGG, 44.1kHz, 16-bit, Stereo
**Duration**: 30 seconds to 3 minutes
**Loop**: Must loop seamlessly

## File Naming Convention

### Music
```
music_[type]_[variation].ogg
Examples:
- music_day_01.ogg
- music_night_01.ogg
- music_menu_main.ogg
```

### Sound Effects
```
sfx_[category]_[name]_[variation].wav
Examples:
- sfx_fishing_cast_01.wav
- sfx_horror_whisper_02.wav
- sfx_ui_button_click.wav
```

### Ambient
```
ambient_[location]_[layer]_[variation].ogg
Examples:
- ambient_calm_lake_base.ogg
- ambient_deep_ocean_rain.ogg
- ambient_cavern_night.ogg
```

## Audio Technical Requirements

### Music Tracks
- **Format**: OGG Vorbis (for streaming)
- **Sample Rate**: 44.1kHz
- **Bit Depth**: 16-bit
- **Channels**: Stereo
- **Compression**: Quality 6-8 (OGG)
- **Loop**: Must loop seamlessly
- **Size**: ~2-5MB per track

### Sound Effects
- **Format**: WAV (for memory loading)
- **Sample Rate**: 44.1kHz or 48kHz
- **Bit Depth**: 16-bit
- **Channels**: Mono (for 3D sounds) or Stereo (for UI)
- **Duration**: 0.1s to 5s
- **Size**: ~50KB to 500KB per sound

### Ambient Layers
- **Format**: OGG Vorbis (for streaming)
- **Sample Rate**: 44.1kHz
- **Bit Depth**: 16-bit
- **Channels**: Stereo
- **Compression**: Quality 6-7 (OGG)
- **Loop**: Must loop seamlessly
- **Duration**: 30s to 3 minutes
- **Size**: ~1-3MB per layer

If you need to test the system before final audio:

1. **Use simple synth tones** (generated in Audacity)
2. **Use free music** from Incompetech

4. **Focus on functionality**, not quality

This allows you to:
- Test audio system functionality
- Verify AudioMixer works
- Test 3D spatial audio
- Verify pooling and ducking
